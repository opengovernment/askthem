# Civic AI & Moderation Partners Research — 2026 Launch

Research on companies and organizations aligned with the MacArthur-Omidyar **Humanity AI** initiative that are active in civic AI systems, AI moderation, and deliberative democracy technology.

## 1. The Humanity AI Initiative — Context

Launched October 14, 2025, **Humanity AI** is a $500M five-year initiative co-chaired by the **MacArthur Foundation** (John Palfrey, President) and **Omidyar Network** (Michele L. Jawando, President). Ten founding philanthropies are committed across five priority areas: **Democracy, Education, Humanities & Culture, Labor & Economy, and Safety & Security**.

Pooled-fund grantmaking via **Rockefeller Philanthropy Advisors** begins in 2026 — making this the ideal moment for aligned civic tech projects to position for partnership.

## 2. High-Priority Partner Companies & Organizations

### Tier 1 — Directly Aligned with Humanity AI / Civic AI Deliberation

#### Cortico (nonprofit) — Cambridge, MA
- **What they do:** AI-powered conversation platform that surfaces underheard voices and perspectives; builds scalable dialogue networks.
- **Key leader:** **Deb Roy** (MIT Media Lab professor, co-founder). Co-authored the "Conversation Networks" paper (2025) with Lawrence Lessig and Audrey Tang.
- **Humanity AI alignment:** Direct grantee territory — civic engagement, democracy pillar. Roy sits on the Knight First Amendment Institute board and Berkman Klein Center.
- **Why partner:** Their conversation-analysis AI and community network of 250+ organizations is infrastructure for inclusive civic deliberation at scale.
- **Link:** https://cortico.ai

#### Pol.is (open source)
- **What they do:** Wiki-survey software for large-group opinion mapping. Algorithmically surfaces consensus rather than engagement/outrage. Anonymous participation to avoid personal attacks.
- **Key leaders:** Colin Megill, Christopher Small, Michael Bjorkegren.
- **Humanity AI alignment:** Used by Taiwan's vTaiwan for policy deliberation; influenced Twitter/X Community Notes; Anthropic explored using Polis for model alignment.
- **Why partner:** The gold standard for AI-assisted large-scale deliberation. Open source, proven at government scale (Taiwan, California).
- **Link:** https://pol.is

#### Humane Intelligence (nonprofit PBC)
- **What they do:** Community-driven AI auditing, bias bounties, red-teaming exercises. Co-organized "Hack the Future" — largest-ever GenAI red-teaming with 8 major tech companies and 4,000 participants.
- **Key leader:** **Dr. Rumman Chowdhury** — TIME 100 AI, BBC 100 Women, Forbes Five Shaping AI. U.S. Science Envoy for AI. Responsible AI Fellow at Harvard Berkman Klein Center.
- **Humanity AI alignment:** Chowdhury was highlighted in Brookings' "(un)Hidden Figures in AI" alongside other civic AI leaders. Deep policy engagement (Congress, FTC, OECD, UNESCO).
- **Why partner:** Leading voice in accountable AI with strong credibility across government, philanthropy, and tech. Red-teaming and auditing expertise directly relevant to trust & safety in civic systems.
- **Link:** https://humane-intelligence.org

#### Reboot Democracy AI / The GovLab
- **What they do:** Research initiative focused on using AI to strengthen participatory democracy. Runs InnovateUS (free AI training for public sector), lectures, and courses on designing democratic engagement.
- **Key leader:** **Beth Simone Noveck** — former White House Deputy CTO under Obama, New Jersey's first Chief AI Strategist. Book: *Reboot: The Race to Save Democracy with AI* (Yale University Press).
- **Humanity AI alignment:** Directly at the intersection of democracy + AI + public sector. Noveck has relationships across the foundation world.
- **Why partner:** Brings academic rigor, government credibility, and a practitioner network of public-sector AI professionals worldwide.
- **Link:** https://rebootdemocracy.ai

#### Data & Society Research Institute
- **What they do:** Launched the **AI Civics** initiative — treats AI as a domain of civic life where people have rights, responsibilities, and avenues for collective action. Seeking partners for civics curricula through 2029.
- **Humanity AI alignment:** Received $500K from MacArthur for civic engagement and public conversations about AI as part of Humanity AI's initial aligned grantmaking.
- **Why partner:** Already funded by Humanity AI coalition. Their AI Civics framework is directly complementary to civic tech platforms.
- **Link:** https://datasociety.net

#### AI Now Institute
- **What they do:** Research institute challenging the current trajectory of AI. Focus on AI governance, national security, and corporate power in AI.
- **Humanity AI alignment:** Received $2M from MacArthur as part of Humanity AI's initial grants.
- **Why partner:** Major credibility in AI policy. Their research shapes regulatory conversations.
- **Link:** https://ainowinstitute.org

### Tier 2 — Civic AI Platforms & Tools

#### Go Vocal (formerly CitizenLab) — Brussels, Belgium
- **What they do:** SaaS community engagement platform for local governments. AI-powered analytics (sentiment detection, topic clustering, trend analysis). Used by Cambridge City Council (UK) and hundreds of EU municipalities.
- **Why partner:** Most commercially mature civic participation platform. Strong EU presence; expanding to North America.
- **Link:** https://www.govocal.com

#### Decidim (open source) — Barcelona origin
- **What they do:** Comprehensive open-source participation platform — debates, proposals, assemblies, budgeting. Experimenting with GPT integration. Democratically governed.
- **Why partner:** Largest open-source civic participation platform globally. Free, modular, and trusted by governments. Natural fit for foundation-funded projects.
- **Link:** https://decidim.org

#### Remesh
- **What they do:** AI-assisted digital deliberation platform. Used for OpenAI's "Democratic Inputs to AI" process (1,500 Americans deliberating on AI guidelines in two weeks). AI helps with prompt suggestions to reduce framing bias.
- **Why partner:** Proven at scale for AI governance deliberation specifically. Strong case study with OpenAI.
- **Link:** https://remesh.ai

#### Make.org — Paris, France
- **What they do:** Civic tech platform for large-scale citizen consultations. Building significant AI features for proposal analysis and synthesis.
- **Why partner:** European civic tech leader. Co-launched Global Coalition for Inclusive AI Governance at Paris AI Action Summit (Feb 2025) with Stanford's Deliberative Democracy Lab.

#### All Our Ideas
- **What they do:** Pairwise voting / opinion prioritization tool. Used by New Jersey's State AI Task Force to engage 2,200 workers in prioritizing AI workforce issues.
- **Why partner:** Simple, accessible tool for priority-setting. Proven in state-level AI governance.
- **Link:** https://allourideas.org

### Tier 3 — Major Tech with Civic AI Divisions

#### Jigsaw (Google)
- **What they do:** Builds tools to mitigate online harms — Perspective API (toxicity detection), content moderation research. Published "Using LLMs to Support Online Communities" (2025).
- **Why partner:** Deep moderation expertise and resources. Perspective API is already widely used in civic platforms. Google's civic tech experiment arm.
- **Link:** https://jigsaw.google.com

#### Anthropic
- **What they do:** AI safety company (Claude). Explored using Polis for democratic model alignment. CEO Dario Amodei is a vocal advocate for democratic AI governance.
- **Humanity AI alignment:** Partnered with Taiwan's Ministry of Digital Affairs on Alignment Assemblies. Amodei's "entente" framework for democratic AI aligns with Humanity AI's democracy pillar.
- **Why partner:** Both a potential technology partner (Claude for deliberation facilitation) and a values-aligned AI company that has demonstrated commitment to democratic governance even at significant cost (DoD contract dispute, Feb 2026).

#### Mozilla Foundation
- **What they do:** Founding member of Humanity AI. Running a **Democracy & AI Cohort** with $50K grants for: information ecosystem resilience, institutional transparency, and civic participation tools.
- **Humanity AI alignment:** Founding coalition member.
- **Why partner:** Already inside the coalition. Their Democracy & AI grantees will be natural collaborators.
