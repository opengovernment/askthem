Key Drivers of Growth

CMS actuaries attribute the elevated spending growth in 2023–2024 to:

  • Increased utilization and intensity — more people seeking care and more intensive treatments
  • High insurance coverage rates — 91.8% of the population insured in 2024
  • Healthcare prices on par with economy-wide inflation — the main driver was volume, not price

Projections: 2024–2033

Looking ahead, CMS projects health spending will continue to outpace the broader economy:

  • Average annual NHE growth of 5.8% vs. GDP growth of 4.3%
  • Health's share of GDP projected to reach 20.3% by 2033
  • Federal share of health spending to rise from 48% to 53%
  • Spending growth may slow to ~5.6% in 2026–27 as enhanced Marketplace subsidies expire
